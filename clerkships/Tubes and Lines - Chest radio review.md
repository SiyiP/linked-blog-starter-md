endotracheal tube
- detector is shooting xray beams from the back towards their front 
- mid-trachea , 4-5 cm above carina, 
- in peds, 1.5 cm above carina bc much smaller trachea 
- we always get an xray after placing it - moves in the pt with their head 
- **chin goes down = tube tip goes down, chin up = tube goes up.**
	- always look at the patient - and keep this in mind!! 
- make sure it's not esophagus - this is easy to miss! 
	- indicators on tube that change color 
	- cxr
		- if u follow the tube it will go below the carina, straight down. sees an esophageal air column 
	- listen to lungs+abdomen 
- if u hear the breath sounds on the R but not the L -> **the right main stem bronchi would be the most common one where the endotracheal tube goes into**

nasograstric tube
- where do we want?  - in the stomach 
- tip of it is 10 cm **beyond gastro esophageal junction** 
- descend midline, following esophagus , cross diaphragm at midline, below left hemidiaphragm 
- **used to decompress stomach in a small bowel obstruction....**
- you want the tip to be also in the stomach, and get it into the "s" shape, the suction needs to go in more. 
- deep into the L lung -> bad pneumonia / aspiration pneumonia 
- hard kink / loop - fracture of the bronchus -> bronchoperforation = flail lung and pneumothorax when you pull it out. need a chest tube. 

feeding tube is **past the pylorus, keeping the feed from getting up thru up the body and aspirate.** = coughing = easy to come up and aspirate 

you might see = **esophageal temperature probes = in the lower 1/3 of esophagus, to temperature track in the esophagus, midline, tip near the mediastinum**

chest tube
- need to be done after needle drain of pneumothorax and pressurizing
- do for pneumothorax and pleural effusion. 
- main complications = laceration of lung and malposition. 
- placed into the pleural space. side holes near the tip of the tube are meant to be in the space of pneumothorax, that's where we're suctioning air. 
	patients laying down - hard to dx pneumothorax bc air's going up in their chest 
	- implore - when i'm on my rounds - make sure that **the patients will pull the patient up to make sure we see pneumothoraces**
- pnuemothorax - black air in the lung, and air black in pleural - **see tiny white line**
- in skin folds, it looks more like a whitish **graduated area, and gas distal. there's lung markings too.**


Central venous catheter 
- subclavian + internal jug =>brachiocephalic vein
- make sure to place it not in valves 
- central lines need to be past the first rib bc avoid the valves
- in the **cavoatrial junction** = **find the carina and go down 2 vertebrae!!!!!**
- can be put thru internal jugular catheter, subclavian catheter, and posterior ICC catheter
- if pulled the central line - you can cause pneumothorax, can thrombose, can perforate, infection 
- no crossing midline!! that could be an. arterial placement!!


swan ganz cath = pulmonary artery catheter now, more proximal  part of pulm artery 

intra aortic balloon pump- help the body pump when there's HF, putting it in the **descending aorta, putting it distal to the aortic arches(great vessels), proximal to the renal arteries!.**
