IV cath in the dorsum of hand - drape over the bed to get more blood into the vessels
- tie the tourniquet above the wrist and re-examine the hand, engorging the vein 
- ensure the extremity positioning below their heart 
- gently tap on the vein by flicking it a bit 
- after identification, tie tourniquet proximal to the insertion, folding one end over the other to make it ez to remove it with one hand. 
- clean with chlorihexadine swab and let it dry 
- elective IV = use local anesthetic, making a small wheal in the subq tissue. 

the bevel faces **upwards, away from their skin when inserting needle + cath**
- gently put the needle + cath at a 40-45 degree angle, and then advance catheter slowly into the vein, watching for blood. 
- when blood happens, decrease catheter angle 
- advance needle and cath together 1 half cm (you must advance the needle very slightly past the first point of flash at a shallow angle)
- gently push the catheter forward while holding the needle steady with thumb and middle finger 
- blood will go into the cath freely if successful, but if there's not, it might be sitting against a venous valve 
- if that's the case, try to move it forward thru the valve, but then if unsuccessful leave for 3-5 min to make a clot form to prevent hematoma 
- after inserting and threading, occlude the proximal end of the catheter by blocking the vein on their hand and then remove the needle 
- untie tourniquet 

post procedure, secure IV tubing onto the catheter by turning it clockwise onto the catheter hub/base.
- watch the IV drip chamber to see if fluid's flowing freely into it 
- put small tape under the catheter hub and fold each side upwards across the hub and then attach to the skin 
- tegaderm is placed over to keep skin clean and dry 
- loop tubing away from insertion site and then tape onto the forearm and wrist.

male catheterization - we see lidocaine jelly , urine collection, and others in the kit 
- put on sterile gloves and cover pt with fenestrated drape. 
- antiseptic solution pour onto cotton balls, and clean that urethral meatus. 
- lubricate cath with 2% lidocaine jelly 
- gently insert tip of cath into the urethra, up to the junction of the inflation port. 
- insert at least 20 cm of cath into the urethra, and obtain the urine into containers. 
- if it's leaft into the bladder, you will blow up the balloon by injecting 10 ccs of sterile water
- gently pull on the catheter to make sure the balloon's in the bladder, stopping cath from slipping out . 
- removing it, first deflat and then slowly slide it out the bladder.

female catheterization 
- lithotomy position 
- cover with the fenestrated drape 
- will use cotton balls again pouring antiseptic onto it 
- separate the labia and clean , using 3-4 will be necessary 
- lube cath with 2% lidocaine jelly 
- gently insert tip of catheter into the uretra, inserting at least 20 cm of this into urethra to ensure the tip is in the bladder. 
- same with the balloon tip and getting it in

PPE
- mask + protective eyewwear or face shields 
- cover both nose + mouth, **tie the knots on the crown of head and around the neck!!!**
- scrub nurse will open the sterile gown onto a small table 
- timed method - counted stroke method - 3 min ? or it's based on individual policy 
- performed with scrub brush with antiseptic solutions (or at sink to add to brush)
- scrub top tucked into the scrub pants, then will adjust mask and PPE BEFORE starting the scrub
1. routine arm and hand wash with antiseptic soap , rinse a lot 
2. unwrap the sterile packet and remove the nail cleaner
3. hold brush and then clean underneath with the toothpick nail cleaner thing. 
4. moisten the sponge with water and antiseptic soap and lather and then clean the nails. 
	1. scrub nails with 30 strokes 
	2. scrub each side of each finger separately and then first on one hand then the other one by one. 
	3. and then palm one side and then the other. scrub all parts of hand separately btw. 
5. proceed to arm, 20 strokes on all 4 surfaces and then the other without returning to previously scrubbed areas. 
6. extend scrub to 2 inch above the elbow, onto your bicep! same deal with the strokes. 
7. avoid splashing water on the scrub suit!!! 
8. keep hands higher than elbows at all times. 
9. first rinse by passing hands first and then the arms under running water, keeping elbows flexed. don't run it back and forth 
10. then enter the OR entering by pushing door open with back. 
11. dry hands well. then get gloved. 
12. lift the towel and then allow the sterile towel to unfold FAST bc you dont want water to drip onto the table. **bend forward making sure the towel doesn't touch the suit**
13. ofc first dry the hands and then dry the 2nd one with blotting technique. 

gowning yourself?
1. gown has inner surface - they are folded inside out . 
2. after drying - grasp gown just below neckline and lift up and away from table. 
3. step away from table and allow gown to unfold. 
4. don't lower the armfolds, just place arms and hands into the sleeves. and then assist with the circulator. 
5. glove with the close technique 
6. ariana grande sleeves maneuvering the gloves out. gloves in the wrapper are inside, take the side pieces of wrapper unfold to reveal them. 
7. invert the folds slightly 
8. position L hand palm upwards, and then pick up glove shielded with right hand and place glove over. 
	1. finger pointing into wrist = oriented correctly 
9. grasp under glove cuff with left and upper glove cuff with right hand still covered. 
10. pull it over, making sure your fingers and thumbs are close to the cuff 

open gloving technique to help in minor procedures 
- open outer nonsterile wrapped and then expose gloves onto the surface similar to the previous thing 
- dont touch the wrapp on the underside of that glove. 

gowning and gloving of other team members 
- allowing formal acknowledgement of the team members are by the surgeon initiated. 
- scrub will hand towel to the main surgeon and then to other members of the team 
**long edge of the towel should be hanging between the hands and arms**

when handing the gown to someone else - 
- cuff hands under the outside of the gown sterile side and then they'll place arms into sleeves. 
- glove member - tuck hands under the outside glove and loop through for the team member.

removing sterile attire - always gown first 
- grab gown under shoulders and then pull away from body to break ties 
- rolls it inside out, and then 
- gloves removed with glove to glove
- bare fingers skin to skin putting it inside the glove and rolling the last one off.
