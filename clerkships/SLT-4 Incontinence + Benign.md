	uterine polyps not seen on exam so we go to **ultrasound or hystoroscopy**
	u can run saline into the cavity to expand the dark areas to visualize the polyp better.

a - adenomyosis - cousin of endometriosis, uterine muscle cells migrate 
- **growth of endometrial glands into surrounding myometrium, within every future periods these endometrial cells respond to the changes -> growth of that myometrium and bleeds, <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">enlarged, globular, single big ball and tender "boggy" uterus on exam!!! if squishy to painful, adenomyosis</mark>
-  hard to prove without surgery ): but **highly suggestive = MRI or imaging, definitive tx = hysterectomy, dx confirmed by histology** ; for our practice = <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">send for an ultrasound, gyn scans should be read.</mark>
- most effective hormonal medicines = mirena iud (hystorecomy is classic treatment. more commonly dx in women past childbearing...)
- for those women dx earlier in life, this can be *cause of miscarriage*, no real way to treat it unfortunately . 
- <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">buzzwords</mark> = multip with regular heavy bleeding + menorrhagia,<mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;"> cycle schedule not changed </mark>though. 
l - leiomyoma
- benign smooth muscle prolif, <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">hormone responsive, may grow in pregnancy and shrink post menopause.</mark>
	- **compare and contrast with polyps which must be removed post menopause due to potential. if they're fibroids on postmenopausal probaby fine**
- very rare = leiomyofibromas who present as fibroids, you need to have that on your differential if there's fast growth. 
- dx = pelvic exam, ultrasound, considering the CBC is heavy bleeding. 
- submucosal, intramural, subserosal, etc.

m - malignancy or hyperplasia 
- postmenopausal female with vaginal bleeding. (12 months + without bleeding, cancer til otherwise, have to get a workup)
	- ultrasound is huge = TVUS to measure <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">endometrial stripe, 4 mm magical number</mark>
		- more than 4 mm ; endometrial biopsy / D+C 
		- less than 4 mm = less than 1% change of malignancy , observe. **no further workup IFF the bleeding resolves. we assume it's just atrophy**
	- going straight to <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">endometrial biopsy in office or D+C in OR</mark>
- pre-menopausal female with <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">obesity, PMOS, oligomenorrhea and AUB</mark>
	- they should get iud to keep lining thinner, progestins every x month / 3rd month to cycle out that uterine lining, etc.

	- term upd8s
		- WHO (used to use): hyperplasia without atypia, hyperplasia with atypia, endometrial adenocardinoma
