Key Steps in the Descending Pathway

1. [**Initiation (Brain Processing):**](https://www.google.com/search?sca_esv=3d98f7656c2b50f6&q=Initiation+%28Brain+Processing%29&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwjf65yUjcORAxU848kDHUcGLyQQxccNegQIJhAD&mstk=AUtExfCG4pJJXBEJVYJEp1doVRJDvbxWZbdkyOYdmWNBF5PyIZhxD9vP6EShhJoe7MBkrTFwVd8vzgvNVitqyuJBowbrsm2vPwUi3RTdwJi8zzD85_2jEECOFFOV1g5XrIxGoYovJ0lKub-5KeMzujJDOu5oN7rcMUwhwUlPwjFoobD8GqNkl4QyROyONfK-QIMduWYIBrWFNFM6hpO628vmm_EcsHT4GoG0Gl8N8O6v3hGOwQ&csui=3) 
    When pain signals reach the brain (via the ascending pathway), areas like the cortex, hypothalamus, and amygdala send signals to the PAG. 
2. [**Midbrain Activation (PAG):**](https://www.google.com/search?sca_esv=3d98f7656c2b50f6&q=Midbrain+Activation+%28PAG%29&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwjf65yUjcORAxU848kDHUcGLyQQxccNegQIJRAD&mstk=AUtExfCG4pJJXBEJVYJEp1doVRJDvbxWZbdkyOYdmWNBF5PyIZhxD9vP6EShhJoe7MBkrTFwVd8vzgvNVitqyuJBowbrsm2vPwUi3RTdwJi8zzD85_2jEECOFFOV1g5XrIxGoYovJ0lKub-5KeMzujJDOu5oN7rcMUwhwUlPwjFoobD8GqNkl4QyROyONfK-QIMduWYIBrWFNFM6hpO628vmm_EcsHT4GoG0Gl8N8O6v3hGOwQ&csui=3) 
    The PAG receives this information and activates neurons that project to the RVM. 
- [**Medulla Relay (RVM):**](https://www.google.com/search?sca_esv=3d98f7656c2b50f6&q=Medulla+Relay+%28RVM%29&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwjf65yUjcORAxU848kDHUcGLyQQxccNegUIkAEQAw&mstk=AUtExfCG4pJJXBEJVYJEp1doVRJDvbxWZbdkyOYdmWNBF5PyIZhxD9vP6EShhJoe7MBkrTFwVd8vzgvNVitqyuJBowbrsm2vPwUi3RTdwJi8zzD85_2jEECOFFOV1g5XrIxGoYovJ0lKub-5KeMzujJDOu5oN7rcMUwhwUlPwjFoobD8GqNkl4QyROyONfK-QIMduWYIBrWFNFM6hpO628vmm_EcsHT4GoG0Gl8N8O6v3hGOwQ&csui=3) 
    Neurons in the RVM rostral ventromedial medulla (like the n<mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">ucleus raphe magnus</mark>) release neurotransmitters, primarily serotonin and norepinephrine. 
- [**Spinal Cord Inhibition:**](https://www.google.com/search?sca_esv=3d98f7656c2b50f6&q=Spinal+Cord+Inhibition&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwjf65yUjcORAxU848kDHUcGLyQQxccNegUIkwEQAw&mstk=AUtExfCG4pJJXBEJVYJEp1doVRJDvbxWZbdkyOYdmWNBF5PyIZhxD9vP6EShhJoe7MBkrTFwVd8vzgvNVitqyuJBowbrsm2vPwUi3RTdwJi8zzD85_2jEECOFFOV1g5XrIxGoYovJ0lKub-5KeMzujJDOu5oN7rcMUwhwUlPwjFoobD8GqNkl4QyROyONfK-QIMduWYIBrWFNFM6hpO628vmm_EcsHT4GoG0Gl8N8O6v3hGOwQ&csui=3) 
    These neurotransmitters travel down the spinal cord to the dorsal horn, where they block the transmission of pain signals from the first-order sensory neuron to the second-order neuron that would send the signal up to the brain. 
- [**Endogenous Opioids:**](https://www.google.com/search?sca_esv=3d98f7656c2b50f6&q=Endogenous+Opioids&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwjf65yUjcORAxU848kDHUcGLyQQxccNegUIkgEQAw&mstk=AUtExfCG4pJJXBEJVYJEp1doVRJDvbxWZbdkyOYdmWNBF5PyIZhxD9vP6EShhJoe7MBkrTFwVd8vzgvNVitqyuJBowbrsm2vPwUi3RTdwJi8zzD85_2jEECOFFOV1g5XrIxGoYovJ0lKub-5KeMzujJDOu5oN7rcMUwhwUlPwjFoobD8GqNkl4QyROyONfK-QIMduWYIBrWFNFM6hpO628vmm_EcsHT4GoG0Gl8N8O6v3hGOwQ&csui=3) 
    This process also triggers the release of endogenous opioids (like enkephalins) in the spinal cord, further binding to receptors and inhibiting pain transmission
